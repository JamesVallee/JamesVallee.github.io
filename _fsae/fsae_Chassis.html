{{ page.title }}

The chassis is the primary structural and safety system of the car, supporting all major subsystems while protecting the driver and meeting Formula SAE safety regulations.

Objectives:

  • Maximize stiffness-to-weight ratio
  • Increase clearance and rule compliance
  • Improve driver safety and ergonomics
4130 Chromoly Steel
69 lbs
2185 Nm/deg

Design Constraints

Finite Element Analysis

  • FEA used to analyze stiffness under torsional loading with displacement plots
  • Iterative geometry changes increased stiffness while reducing or maintaining weight
  • Peak stresses were identified with stress plots

Jig and Manufacturing

Precise manufacturing was achieved using a CNC MDF jig system and aluminum positioning blocks on a laser cut precision table.
